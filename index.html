for an eye

To the world, Sheikh Hasina speaks of peace
and healing. From her loyalists, she asks for
violent revenge.

Aaqib Md Shatil
June 13, 2026

Following her fall in a mass uprising in 2024, Sheikh Hasina continues to call for revenge as her supporters claim they have fallen victim to violence in Bangladesh.

Sheikh Hasina, who has recently been sentenced to death for her command responsibilities over the crimes against humanity committed during the 2024 Bangladesh uprising, has been urging her supporters to seek revenge, burn down the houses of her political opponents and instigating the military to use the tanks like they used in 1975, according to the phone conversations with her followers that are making rounds on social media after being leaked.

Sheikh Hasina and her party, the Awami League, ruled Bangladesh from January 2009 until August 5, 2024. Critics of her administration allege that Bangladesh devolved into a “personalised autocracy,” during which over two and a half thousand people were killed in staged crossfires, and several hundred, predominantly opposition party members, went missing.

Sheikh Hasina vowed revenge against the plaintiffs. In a conversation with Jahangir Alam, a leader from her party in the Gobindganj area of northern Bangladesh, which was leaked just two months after her downfall in October 2024, she told Alam that she had ordered to prepare a list of the 227 individuals who filed the cases against her to murder them. She stated, “I got the license to kill at least 227.”

Following the fall of the regime, hundreds of Awami League supporters, many of whom were allegedly involved in suppressing dissenters for years, were subjected to revenge attacks by their rivals. The leader of the Awami League instructed her followers to retaliate proportionately.

In at least three recordings, posted on Facebook and YouTube, Sheikh Hasina was found instructing her party men to make a list and create a database of the ones who attacked her followers and filed cases against her so that she can exact vengeance accordingly. “I'm creating a list of everyone who was beaten (or killed),” she said in a call obtained on October 29th 2025.

The suggestion to use fire for committing violence appeared frequently in her private conversations. In response to the stories of local Awami League leaders’ houses being attacked that emerged, she ordered to ‘take action against those who have harmed’ them. “If you touch fire, your hand will burn [...] What else should I say?” she threatened.

Calling the interim government led by Muhammad Yunus, which took over the country after she fled, illegal, in another leaked call from September 2025, she urged her supporters to avenge without waiting for the right time.

Being aware that her supporters, while committing violence, may get rounded up by the locals or the police, the deposed prime minister urged her followers to use guerrilla warfare tactics reminiscent of Bangladesh’s liberation war.

A former minister in her cabinet was also seen promoting armed struggle similar to the 1971 Liberation War. Mohibul Hasan Chowdhury claimed that their party men would launch a war using the tactics used in 1971. “If we’re left with no other option, we will have to prepare for a second liberation war.”

On at least two occasions, Sheikh Hasina justified the violence of her party’s supporters by referring to the Islamic concept of Qisas, a form of retributive justice, from the holy Quran.

Although on one hand, Sheikh Hasina criticised the interim government for ‘seizing’ power illegally, she herself instigated the armed forces in at least two addresses to her followers.

After the International Crimes Tribunal charged dozens of military officials for their involvement in enforced disappearances, Sheikh Hasina was also found instigating the Bangladesh armed forces to rebel and use their tanks. Reminding the military officials that the tanks were once used to kill her family members, she expressed her frustration: “They (the tanks) were used to kill us. But they can’t use them now.”

She questioned whether the armed forces have any capability to take action against those who protect the sovereignty of Bangladesh, as they sat idle when two leaders from the newly formed student-led National Citizen Party (NCP) threatened them. “Has the army lost its strength? Can’t they arrest them and take them away? Then they would’ve gotten an answer,” she asked the audience.

The possibility appeared to be frail as Sheikh Hasina continued to show no remorse whatsoever for the death of over seven hundred individuals during the crackdown she and her home minister presided over. Without showing a semblance of remorse for the atrocities committed under her watch, she claimed that she had found a new mission, in a leaked call made available on October 29th 2025, where, labelling her political opposition as ‘dirt’, she pledged that she would ‘cleanse the dirt’.
